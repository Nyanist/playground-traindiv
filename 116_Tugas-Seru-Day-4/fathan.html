Orion

Orion is one of the most recognizable constellations, named for the mythological hunter in Greek myth. It is prominent in the winter sky and is visible from most of the world because it lies near the celestial equator. Key features include the three bright stars of Orion's Belt and prominent stars like the red supergiant Betelgeuse and the blue supergiant Rigel. The Orion Nebula, a region of star formation, is also located in the constellation

Scorpion

Scorpio is a zodiac constellation located in the southern celestial hemisphere, named for the scorpion and known for its bright stars and distinct hook-like shape. It is most visible during the summer in the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by the bright red supergiant star Antares, which is considered the "heart" of the scorpion
